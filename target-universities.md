# Johns Hopkins University
- **Strong Majors:** Offers BS/BA in Computer Science, majors in engineering (e.g. Electrical, Computer) and a minor in Entrepreneurship (via the Center for Leadership Education)【12†L10-L16】【9†L9-L11】. Ideal for CS/AI with research opportunities (e.g. undergraduate research labs) and growing entrepreneurship support (e.g. FastForward incubator).
- **Admissions (Intl):** Apply via Common App or Coalition (hopkins.app)【14†L283-L290】. Requires high school transcript (grades 9–11, plus 12th mid-year/final), counselor report, two teacher recs, personal essay, activities list, and optional supplemental materials【14†L292-L300】【14†L312-L320】. Standardized tests *are required* (holistic review; test scores considered in context)【14†L259-L266】. No strict minimum GPA; admitted class average GPA ~3.95 (unweighted)【27†L182-L186】.
- **Standardized Tests:** SAT or ACT strongly recommended. Recent admitted class middle 50% SAT ~1530–1570【27†L192-L194】. (JHU will holistically consider self-reported/official scores.)
- **English:** Non-native speakers are *recommended* (not strictly required) to submit TOEFL/IELTS/Duolingo/Cambridge scores【23†L219-L227】. Competitive scores: TOEFL iBT ≥100 (with subscore targets), IELTS ≥7.0 (each band), Duolingo ≥120【23†L231-L237】. Can waive if ≥4 years in English-instructing school.
- **Deadlines (Fall 2027):** Early Decision I — Nov 1, 2026 (notification Dec), Early Decision II — Jan 2, 2027, Regular — Jan 2, 2027【1†L189-L192】. Financial aid deadlines 2–3 weeks later. Apply through CommonApp/Coalition portal【1†L189-L194】【14†L283-L290】.
- **Requirements & Portal:** Submit CommonApp with Hopkins questions, plus Hopkins portal for optional uploads【14†L283-L290】. Submit *international* Certification of Finances and CSS Profile if requesting aid【14†L328-L336】.
- **Scholarships:** Need-based financial aid is available to internationals (must apply with CSS Profile)【25†L359-L364】. A **very limited** number of merit scholarships are awarded to international applicants (no extra app needed)【25†L369-L378】. Families meeting income criteria pay no tuition (financial aid), but merit awards for intl are rare.
- **Costs:** Tuition ~$59,000/year; with room/board ~$20K. (Hopkins meets full need for all students admitted, but cap on intl merit aid.)
- **Co-op/Internships:** Extensive Baltimore/DC tech ecosystem; ~40% of students do internships. Hopkins's Life Design Lab and career services connect students to internships at tech firms and startups. Many undergrads conduct research.
- **Entrepreneurship:** Strong support via the Center for Leadership Education and FastForward accelerator. Hopkins hosts hackathons, startup weekend, and alumni networks in biotech/tech.
- **Selectivity:** Very selective (~7–8% acceptance recently) – among *most competitive* U.S. universities. Admitted students typically in top 10% of class, ~3.95 GPA【27†L180-L186】.
- **Fit for Profile:** **Excellent academically** for high-achieving tech students, with top-ranked engineering/CS programs and research. Pros: Leading CS/engineering faculty, innovation labs, strong funding for research. Cons: High competition, very low admission chances, expensive, limited merit aid for internationals.
- **CS/Engineering Ranking:** Top 10 nationally in CS/Engineering. Known for research strength (e.g. AI, biomedical engineering) and leading graduate programs.
- **Undergrad Research:** Hopkins encourages UREU (Undergrad Research Awards), lab work in CS/AI fields, plus programs like Johns Hopkins EDGE for entrepreneurship.
- **Location:** Baltimore, MD – urban campus with growing tech scene, near D.C. biotech/tech hubs. Opportunities via proximity to government and tech companies.

# Northeastern University
- **Strong Majors:** BS/MS in Computer Science, programs in AI/CS; minors in entrepreneurship and robust co-op engineering programs. Top for experiential learning (e.g. co-ops in tech firms).
- **Admissions (Intl):** Apply via Common App or Coalition; $75 fee. Submit high school transcript (with certified English translation if needed), one counselor and one teacher recommendation, personal essay, activities. Northeastern is *test-optional*: SAT/ACT not required【35†L197-L206】. If submitted, they superscore across ACT/SAT. Average SAT for matriculants typically ~1400–1500. Must demonstrate English proficiency if non-native (see below).
- **Standardized Tests:** Optional. All applicants (US and international) may submit or not without penalty【35†L197-L206】. Admissions unaffected by lack of scores.
- **English:** All applicants must show English proficiency. Accepts TOEFL, IELTS, Duolingo, PTE, or Cambridge C1/C2. (Waiver possible if 4 years prior schooling in English.) TOEFL typically ≥80, IELTS ≥6.5. Must submit scores unless waived【35†L219-L227】【35†L228-L236】.
- **Deadlines (Fall 2027):** Early Decision I — Nov 1, 2026; Early Decision II — Jan 1, 2027; Early Action — Dec 1, 2026; Regular — Jan 1, 2027【36†L31-L35】【33†L207-L208】. Financial aid: CSS/Fafsa deadlines ED1 Nov 15, EA Dec 1, ED2 Jan 15, RD Feb 15【33†L237-L244】.
- **Requirements & Portal:** Apply via CommonApp/Coalition. After submission, use Northeastern's Application Status portal for transcripts and optional documents. International applicants must submit Declaration of Finances (online)【38†L169-L177】. Northeastern is need-aware for internationals (cost of attendance is considered in admission).
- **Scholarships:** International students are automatically considered for merit scholarships upon admission. Award examples: **International Scholars Award** (up to $28,000/year)【38†L205-L212】. No separate scholarship app is needed (merit determined by admission review). Need-based aid *not* available to internationals.
- **Costs:** Tuition ~$33,995 per quarter (≈$136K/yr)【39†L168-L171】. Estimated living/fees ~$20K/yr extra. Northeastern Promise program offers some merit aid to high-achievers (mostly domestic), but few need-based funds for intl.
- **Co-op/Internships:** Signature co-op program: alternating 6-month full-time work experiences (often in tech companies in Boston, SF, etc.). >90% of students complete at least one co-op; career outcomes are excellent (avg. mid-50% starting salary $70K+). Many co-ops at major tech firms, startups.
- **Entrepreneurship:** Active entrepreneurship ecosystem: IDEA accelerator, Center for Entrepreneurship Education, Husky Startup Challenge, and startup culture in Boston. Strong tech and startup networking in Boston/Cambridge.
- **Selectivity:** Moderately selective (undergrad admit rate ~15-20%). Students typically above-average grades (mid-50% GPA ~3.6, SAT 1370-1500). Very strong resumes/co-op profile can boost odds.
- **Fit for Profile:** **Strong fit for tech/startup interests**. Pros: Outstanding co-op program yielding industry experience; vibrant Boston tech scene; research opportunities (esp. via undergrad honors programs); top entrepreneurship resources. Cons: Very expensive tuition; need-aware admissions; less prestige than Ivy-equivalent schools; co-op may extend graduation timeline.
- **CS/Engineering Ranking:** Northeastern engineering and CS programs rank in the national top 40. Known for interdisciplinary innovation and industry ties.
- **Undergrad Research:** Available through Undergraduate Research and Fellowships Office, honors theses, and faculty labs (especially in cybersecurity, AI, biotech).
- **Location:** Boston, MA – major tech hub (hub of biotech, software, finance), strong startup ecosystem (like Kendall/MIT area).

**Overall Ranking & Fit:** Johns Hopkins and Northeastern clearly stand out for academic prestige and tech ecosystems (Dream schools). Ranks near them include UCF and Rutgers for CS programs. Mid-tier targets: ASU, Stony Brook, Kansas, Drexel—good tech resources and moderate selectivity. Safeties: UNH, Memphis, Kansas State, Idaho—more accessible with specialized strengths.

- **Dream:** Johns Hopkins, Northeastern (very low admit rates, high profile, strong research/co-op).
- **Target:** ASU, UCF, Rutgers, Stony Brook, Cincinnati, Drexel, Kansas (respectable programs, solid fit).
- **Safe:** UNH, Memphis, Kansas State, Idaho (more affordable, easier admission, but fewer high-profile resources).

**Best ROI/Startup/Internships:** Northeastern and UCF offer outstanding ROI via co-op placements and Orlando tech jobs. Rutgers and ASU have strong startup support (Silicon Desert in AZ, and NJ/NYC access). UCF and Stony Brook benefit from local industries.

**Best for Startups:** Northeastern (Boston incubators), ASU (Tempe's SkySong), UCF (Orlando/space-tech culture), JHU (FastForward), Drexel (Philly biotech).

**Highest Admission Chances & Aid:** Kansas State, University of Idaho, UNH, Memphis have relatively open admission and offer some int'l scholarships, making them safest for admission and aid.

**Strongest Course Suggestions:** CS or Software Engineering are safe fits everywhere; consider universities' flagship programs (e.g. UCF's CS/Optics, ASU's engineering, KU's Eng; JHU's CS). At each campus, lean towards CS/AI or engineering tracks with entrepreneurship minors/certs for your startup interests.

**Conclusion:** For an ambitious Indian student aiming at tech and startups, **Johns Hopkins and Northeastern** are top-tier aspirational picks (Dreams). **ASU, UCF, Stony Brook, Rutgers, and Drexel** are excellent Targets balancing reputation and ecosystem. More accessible **Safeties** include Kansas, UNH, Memphis, Iowa State, Idaho—lower cost/risk but decent tech offerings. Pursue campuses with vibrant co-op and entrepreneurship cultures (especially Northeastern, UCF, ASU, Drexel) to maximize internships and startup exposure.
